### English Version

I’ve been thinking about something a lot lately:

If AI is going to participate more deeply in our work, decisions, and expression, then we cannot keep letting our memory, judgment, workflows, and accumulated experience be defined by closed platform silos.

That’s why I started building `MoonOS`.
Not to make another AI shell, but to seriously ask:
**Can we build an AI asset control plane that truly belongs to users?**

In `MoonOS`, memory is not disposable context, workflows are not trapped inside a single vendor format, and decisions are not treated as permanent truth just because they were written down once.

I want them to be:
- portable
- governable
- falsifiable
- co-built in the open

This is not something one person should define alone.
If you care about questions like:
- how AI memory can actually remain under user control
- how prompts / skills / workflows can escape platform lock-in
- how AI judgments can be reviewed, governed, and corrected
- how we avoid turning our own bias into stronger machine bias

please take a look — and if it resonates, help build it with me.
Questions, criticism, ideas, and PRs are all welcome.
Even pointing out where the direction is wrong would be valuable.

Open source repo: `https://github.com/zzyong24/moonos`

If you feel this matters, I’d really appreciate a repost.
I’m trying to find the people who care deeply about this problem, and build it together.
